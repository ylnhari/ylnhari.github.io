From mainframes to machine learning

A rare engineer who has worked both ends of the enterprise stack — the COBOL/Db2 systems that run businesses, and the AI systems that reinvent them.

I started where the world's most critical business logic still lives — mainframes, COBOL, JCL and Db2 — learning how real enterprises actually keep the lights on at scale.

That foundation became my edge. I moved into data science and machine learning, and then into the discipline that makes ML actually matter: MLOps — reproducible pipelines, automated training, monitoring and clean deployment.

Today I'm driving AI innovation at Best Buy, working across Generative AI, LLMs, time-series forecasting, recommendation systems and optimization. I care about systems that survive contact with production: observable, retrainable, and trusted by the people who depend on them.

Off the clock, I'm a relentless builder and learner — shipping local-first AI tools, writing about what I learn, and grinding algorithms for fun.
